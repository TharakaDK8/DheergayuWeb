What is Dheergayu?

Depression is identified as one of the most common mental health disorders in the world. According to the World Health Organization (WHO) report in 2020, around 264 million suffer from depression disorders around the world. Depression not only impacts the patient but also their families and relatives. If not properly treated, due to these reasons it leads people to hazardous situations. Nonetheless existing clinical diagnosis tools for monitoring illness trajectory are inadequate.

Traditionally, psychiatrists use one to one interaction assessments for diagnosis. However, these clinic-centered services can pose several operational challenges. For monitoring depressive disorders, patients are required to travel regularly to a clinical center within its limited hours of operation and these procedures are highly resource intensive because they require skilled clinician and laboratories. To address these issues, we propose personal and ubiquitous sensing technologies, such as fitness trackers and smartphones. which can monitor human vitals in an unobtrusive manner. In recent years, the sensing ability of mobiles and fitness trackers significantly increased. Image processing and machine learning techniques have been used to analyze user contextual data such as heart rate and blood pressure variances, sleeping pattern variances and static and dynamic facial features. By this proposed system we are planning to bridge the gap between high performance modern sensing technology devices and traditional mental health diagnosis steps into a new pre-emptive era.

Research GAP

As a result of the literature survey, we identified Clinical Depression detection has been done in several ways. They have used Heart Rate variance and Blood Pressure, Sleeping patterns, Static facial features variance and Dynamic facial features variance as depression monitoring areas. But there are more gaps still need to be filled with doing research in these areas. Problems with the existing systems Previous systems only detect the depression on patients. But they do not provide a continuous monitoring of depression level with providing accurate results considering previous data.


  • ●   Previous systems have used medical devices, EEG wearables, and clinical health actigraphy to measure depression. Need of laboratories, special services and special knowledge on usage of equipment makes depression detection process more costly. Because of that reason many patients cannot get the advantage of improvements in technology to get treatments and have a better life by recovering illnesses without spending more money.

  • ●   When using static images to detect depression level, occlusions due to eyewear, shadows and hairstyle can be happen when using the system in a real world platform. Because of that reason some important landmarks can be lost, and it effects to the accuracy of the depression detection model. Current research does not have a proper solution to that problem.

  • ●   Current applications do not have a proper analyzing method of body movements of a patient.

As the analysis of context of psychiatry research field, need to provide prediction algorithms-based depression detection monitoring assistant using multiple health data models to identify depression status by using key pillar of Machine Learning and Image Processing approaches


Research Problem

Depression and anxiety disorders are highly prevalent worldwide. According to the world health organization, depression is the most common illness worldwide and the leading cause of disability [1]. From the survey conducted in the USA it was validated that 50 - 70 million people suffer [19] from sleep disturbances and other depressive disorders. In the current situation some people haven’t idea about depression and haven relevant way to calculate depression level in real-time. However current depression analyzing and diagnosing very objective and not effective at real-world scenarios, some of the accessing methods [20][21], needs laboratory facilities and expensive diagnosis equipment’s to depression detection. These mentioned methods are not practical with dealing these numbers of patient. To overcome this issue, we planned a solution with fitness tracker. However, fitness trackers usages tend users to healthier life but not ability detect early signs of depression stages.


Research Objectives

  • ●   To create a software to help monitoring clinical depression level using patient’s health data and facial expressions.

  • ●   To improve decisions of clinical depression level using pulse-rate/ blood pressure health data

  • ●   To improve decisions of clinical depression level using static facial features (image analysis)

  • ●   To improve decisions of clinical depression level using sleeping patterns analyzing component

  • ●   To improve decisions of clinical depression level using dynamic facial features (video analysis)

Methodology

This project is about developing a clinical depression assistant which gives depression severity status based on Heart Rate Variability [12], Cyclic Alternation Pattern [13] and Random Eye Movement [14] of sleep, Static facial features [15] and Dynamic facial features [16] of a patient. The stated patient data access from the patient's fitness tracker and Camera of a smartphone via developed application. Under the methodology, reviews the analysis of each health data of a patient, to produce suggestions and further recommendations to improve decision making for caretakers and doctors. The goal of this project design is, assist patients in their difficult situations with the help of minimum user interactions.

Analysis of Dynamic Facial Features

Eye aspect ratio concept which defined in the “Real-Time Eye Blink Detection using Facial Landmarks” research paper which based on the work by Soukupova and Cech [22] used to identify the blinks of the depressed person. This feature only extracts the eye of the provided video and each eye is represented using six coordinates. Fig. 4 Sample eye-blink capturing image, In respectively into p1-p6 facial coordinates. The EAR method used for this feature is mentioned below, EAR value is constant when the eye is open, but the value will drop to zero when a blink occurs. By using the landmark distance (1) this feature has ability to identify the blinks of the depressed person. After identifying the blink count this feature should identify whether this blink count is normal or related to depression. According to [23] depressed people have a low blink rate. It has been reported that the normal spontaneous blink rate is between 12 and 15/min [24]. By getting these mentioned points this feature will generate a report that indicates the depression rate by using the blink rate.


Analysis of Heart Rate Variability

In HRV detection, HRV is the physiological phenomenon of the variation in the time interval between consecutive heartbeats in milliseconds [27]. Using logistic regression model analysis, the HRV, based on the age, gender, blood pressure and HRV rate. Model used fitness band data for its predictions. Model provides 94% of accuracy of the training dataset. According to [28] depression patients have a lower HRV than normal people. and HRV depends on the age limit of the person.
